### Reflection Questions

**Did you achieve your goals last year? Why or why not?**
```
I partially achieved my goals. My target was 22 transactions and I closed 18. I hit my GCI goal of $120K but fell short on volume. The main reason was inconsistent prospecting during Q2 when I got busy with closings and stopped my daily outreach. I also lost two listings to competitors who had stronger listing presentations. On the positive side, my average commission increased because I focused on higher-priced homes in the newer subdivisions.
```

**What were your biggest struggles?**
```
Time management was my biggest struggle. I found myself constantly reactive instead of proactive - answering texts at all hours, showing homes on weekends when I should have been with family, and neglecting lead generation when I had active deals. I also struggled with setting boundaries with demanding clients who expected 24/7 availability. Technology was another pain point - I tried three different CRM systems and never fully committed to any of them.
```

**What was your biggest accomplishment?**
```
My biggest accomplishment was building a consistent social media presence that actually generated business. I committed to posting 3 times per week on Instagram and by Q4, I was getting 2-3 inbound leads per month directly from social media. I also closed my first $600K+ listing which gave me confidence to pursue the move-up market. Getting a 5-star review from a difficult client who initially wanted to cancel our agreement felt like a major win.
```

**How did you prospect last year?**
```
My prospecting was inconsistent but included: calling my sphere of influence about once per quarter (should be monthly), door knocking in my farm area sporadically (maybe 20 times total), hosting 4 open houses, attending 2 networking events per month, and posting on social media. I relied too heavily on Zillow leads which were expensive and low quality. My best results came from past client referrals and sphere calls, but I didn't do enough of either consistently.
```

**What went well that you want to continue?**
```
Social media marketing - I finally found my voice and built engagement. Past client follow-up calls - every time I called someone, good things happened. Open houses in the newer subdivisions - they attracted serious buyers. Partnering with my favorite lender for co-branded marketing. Morning routine of reviewing my goals before checking email. Using video in my listing presentations - it helped me win more listings.
```

```
1. Close 30 transactions (up from 18) with a GCI of $175,000

4. Lose 15 pounds and exercise 4x per week
5. Take a real 2-week vacation with my family
6. Launch a monthly client appreciation event program
7. Earn my GRI designation by year end
```

**Why are these goals important to you?**
```
30 transactions at $175K GCI will give me the financial cushion to hire help and take real time off - it's the tipping point from surviving to thriving. The morning prospecting routine is the keystone habit that will make everything else possible - when I prospect consistently, my pipeline is full and I'm less stressed. The assistant will buy back 10+ hours weekly that I can redirect to dollar-productive activities or family. Health matters because I want to be around for my kids and have energy to serve my clients. The vacation is non-negotiable for my marriage and my sanity.
```

**How do you plan to work towards achieving these goals?**
```
For 30 transactions: Implement the Power Agent prospecting system with daily accountability tracking. Block 8-10am every weekday for lead generation before anything else. Reactivate my past client database with monthly touches. Commit to 2 listing appointments per week minimum.

For the assistant: Start documenting all my processes now. Set aside funds monthly for hiring. Interview candidates in Q1, hire by April.

For health: Join a gym near my office. Meal prep on Sundays. No phone in bedroom. Schedule workouts like appointments.

For vacation: Book it NOW for August before I talk myself out of it.
```

**What are 1-3 steps you can take right away?**
```
1. Tomorrow morning: Block 8-10am on my calendar for the entire year as "Prospecting - Do Not Schedule" and set up my tracking spreadsheet.

2. This week: Pull a list of past clients I haven't contacted in 90+ days and schedule calls to 5 of them.

3. This weekend: Clean out the spare room to create a dedicated home office space, set up a proper workstation, and remove all distractions.
```

### Obstacles and Strategies

**What potential obstacles or challenges do you foresee?**
```
1. Getting pulled into "urgent" client requests during prospecting time
2. Market conditions - interest rates and inventory affecting buyer activity
3. Shiny object syndrome - jumping to new lead sources instead of mastering current ones
4. Self-doubt when I have a slow month
5. Family pushback on increased work hours needed to hit higher goals
6. Finding and affording a good assistant
7. My own resistance to change and tendency to fall back into old habits
```

**What strategies or actions can you take to overcome these obstacles?**
```
1. Prospecting time: Phone goes on airplane mode 8-10am. Tell all clients upfront about my schedule. Use auto-responder: "I return calls after 10am."

2. Market conditions: Focus on what I can control - my activity. Diversify to include investors who buy regardless of rates.

3. Shiny objects: Commit to ONE lead gen method for 90 days before evaluating. No new tools until current ones are mastered.

4. Self-doubt: Daily affirmations. Celebrate small wins. Call my accountability partner when I'm spiraling.

5. Family: Have honest conversation NOW about the investment required and the payoff (vacation, assistant, more flexibility later). Schedule specific family times that are protected.

6. Assistant: Start with virtual assistant for 10 hours/week to test the concept. Reinvest first commission increase into hiring.

7. Habits: Join the Power Agent weekly accountability group. Track daily habits visibly. Reward consistency, not just results.
```
